Free beer and pizza at the LeicesterJS meet up

On the 6th September we attended the inaugural LeicesterJS meet up at CloudCall in the city centre.

We were met at the door by representatives from CloudCall who showed us the way and welcomed us with a fridge full of beers!

Having been to similar meet ups in the education sector, it was an unexpected addition, and set the tone for a more casual evening of discussion.

There seemed to be several pockets of people attending who already knew each other from various projects or previous meet ups. However, our host Josh Ghent, did a great job at making us feel welcome in the time before the talks started.

The room quickly filled up with only two or three empty seats when our first speaker, Craig Russell, took to the podium.

What struck me most about the event, was what a brilliant move by CloudCall it was. After the branded pizza arrived I was left wondering who had footed the bill. Throughout the event we were reminded that CloudCall are hiring and from what we had seen we were very impressed - what a clever way to get professionally engaged people to be aware of your company. The staff at CloudCall were there to talk favourably about their experiences of working with the company and share the exciting technologies they were able to work with. For the price of a pizza and beer, we had out of hours training for staff and a recruitment drive. An exceedingly worthwhile move and something worth replicating in the future!

Overall definitely an evening worth attending, and a huge success considering it was the first meeting. On top of that, I only annoyed Adam once with my doodles in my notebook.
